Banana Pancakes

Description

Golden, fluffy, and infused with the natural sweetness of ripe bananas, these pancakes are a comforting breakfast classic with a British twist. Melted butter adds richness to the batter, while a hint of cinnamon lifts the flavour. Cooked to a crisp edge and soft centre, they’re perfect stacked high and drizzled with maple syrup. Top with fresh banana slices or berries for a vibrant finish. Ideal for slow mornings or a cosy brunch at home.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups plain flour
  • 2 tablespoons caster sugar
  • 2½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 pinch cinnamon (optional)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 2 medium ripe bananas (mashed)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g unsalted butter (melted), plus extra for cooking

Recipe

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Combine wet ingredients: In another bowl, mash the bananas. Add eggs, milk, and melted butter. Stir until smooth.
  3. Make the batter: Pour the dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Stir gently until just combined—don’t overmix. Let the batter rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Cook: Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small knob of butter. Spoon in ¼ cup of batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on top, flip, and cook for another 1–2 minutes until golden.
  5. Serve: Stack them high, drizzle with maple syrup or honey, and top with fresh banana slices or berries.
